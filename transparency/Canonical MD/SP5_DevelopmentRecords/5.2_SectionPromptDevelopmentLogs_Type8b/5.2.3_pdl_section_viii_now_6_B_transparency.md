**Development Process**
PDL-001: Structural Repositioning Decision
Trigger: User examination "where in the overall paper should this actually go?"
Context: Philosophical framework developed in SP4.7.5 was initially intended for Section VII conclusion ("Clarifying the Foundation")
Three options analyzed:
Option A: Within Section VII (Part of Ecological Validity)
Pros: Makes principle immediately concrete, natural
Cons: Might overwhelm section, unbalance principles, interrupt flow
Option B: Opening of Section VIII
Pros: Motivates requirements before introducing them, answers "why THESE components?", prevents importing wrong frameworks, better reader experience (why → what → how)
Cons: Section VIII guidance says "NOT: Re-argue why transparency needed"
Option C: End of Section VII (After Three Principles)
Pros: Synthesizes abstraction, completes framework, natural transition
Cons: Might feel like addition, make Section VII end-heavy
Reader psychology assessment:
Readers hate justification when they want specification
Need PURPOSE before REQUIREMENTS
Key question timing: "But what's this all FOR?" - ask this entering Section VIII
Decision: Option B (Opening of Section VIII)
Rationale:
Normative grounding should precede specification
Reader needs to understand PURPOSE (values) before REQUIREMENTS (disclosure components)
Better section role clarity: VII = design principles, VIII = values → requirements
Prevents importing wrong frameworks (moral desert, economic, pure accountability) right when encountering requirements
Natural flow: principles (VII) → values motivating requirements (VIII opening) → requirements (VIII body)
Structural implications:
Section VII: Remove clarification section, stays ~1,200-1,400 words, focused on three principles
Section VIII: Opens with values framework (~300 words), then specifies components, ~1,400-1,600 words total
Smooth progression without redundancy
Cross-reference: User reflection: "thinking more carefully about it, I feel this makes more sense in the opening of 8, so the person understands which normative orientation is relevant to judging the concrete proposal."

Option B: Compressed Opening (~300 words)
