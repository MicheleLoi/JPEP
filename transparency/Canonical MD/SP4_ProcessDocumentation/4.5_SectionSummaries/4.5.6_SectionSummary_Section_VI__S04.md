### Section IX: Review Mechanism

**Target Length:** 1,200-1,500 words

  

**Function:** Detail split-reviewer system with thorough discussion of reproduction requirement's dual purposes

  

**Structure:**

  

*Overview (~200 words):*

- A. Submission: Paper + full methodological disclosure

- B. Split review:

  - Reviewer A: Reviews paper WITHOUT methodological disclosure

  - Reviewer B: Reviews methodological disclosure WITHOUT paper initially

- C. Reviewer B's dual role:

  - Assess methodological disclosure for scientific quality

  - Test robustness: Reproduce procedure with state-of-art LLM

  - Produce independent review of resulting paper

- D. Editor: Checks coherence between two independent reviews

  

*Purpose 1: Sufficiency Testing (~400 words):*

  

**Core Question:** Is author's input sufficient to generate intellectual advancement valued by review (abstracting from stylistic details)?

  

**Three Metaphors:**

1. **Information Theory:** Prompts as compressed information "unzipped" by LLM. Test checks whether compressed version contains essential intellectual content.

  

2. **Biological:** Author's input = seed; LLM = soil (compressed summary of digitized discourse). Author's role sufficient for causing knowledge output, but only in context of this particular soil.

  

3. **Analytic Philosophy:** [Paper should develop or suggest appropriate metaphor for analytic conventions]

  

**Key Point:** NOT about whether LLM "could have" produced work independently. About whether author's specific inputs functionally determine substantive contribution when operating through this medium.

  

*Purpose 2: Anchoring Authorship (~400 words):*

  

**Core Claim:** Reproduction test establishes author genuinely responsible for main ideas through meaningful control, not mere endorsement.

  

**Key Distinction:** Author must control "the difference that makes the difference"—the right difference.

  

**Distinguishes from Two Failure Modes:**

1. Mere endorsement: Passively accepting whatever LLM produces

2. Chance: Accidentally stumbling on good output through trial and error

  

**When Reviewer B reproduces core intellectual contribution from prompts:**

- Demonstrates author exercised meaningful causal control over outcome

- Author responsible not despite AI mediation but through intentional structuring of that mediation

- Validates that prompts contained the essential intellectual work

  

*Connection to Section VI (~200 words):*

  

**How Reproduction Enables Quality Improvement:**

- Not just accountability—generates feedback on methodology

- Reviewer learns what prompting strategies produce insights vs. dead ends

- Where human intervention essential vs. perfunctory

- Community accumulates knowledge about effective AI-assisted philosophy

- Creates dataset for improving both human practice and AI systems

  

**How This Raises the Bar:**

- High-quality AI work requires conceptual precision in prompts

- Sustained dialogue and iterative refinement

- Can't be casual prompting—must demonstrate genuine intellectual control

- Becomes high-cost signal of methodological commitment

- Opposite of "AI slop"

  

*Conclusion (~100 words):*

- Split review validates both argument quality and methodological rigor

- Reproduction test serves dual purpose: sufficiency + authorship anchoring

- Enables rather than merely polices transparency

- Implements Section VI's vision of quality improvement through feedback
